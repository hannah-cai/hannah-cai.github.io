Paus — conversational AI

p5.js, After Effects / partners: Rachel Park and Cindy Zhang

"Create a concept for a conversational AI. Using p5.js or a similar creative coding library, design and prototype the visuals for your AI."

—

Brainstorming

Our first task was to create a use case for our conversational AI. As a group, we thought of several concepts; initially, we wanted to do a mood tracker, but we eventually decided to focus less on tracking mood and more about having conversations about emotions. Our final concept was "Paus," a conversational AI meant to be a companion to people with anxiety.

Form Exploration

After our concept was set, we explored different visual forms our conversational AI could take. We wanted our AI to have a simple, abstract form. In the end, we decided to try prototyping a sphere which would change texture to respond to input or indicate different statuses.

Iteration 1

I started prototyping in p5 (I did most of the code for this project, since I had the most experience). This first iteration is a 2d circle with variable diameter, based largely off of code from here. While it was fairly close to the form we wanted, we weren't satisfied with the motion, or the fact that we could only have one flat color.

WEBGL colors

I started exploring WEBGL as an alternative. WEBGL a 3d library that can be used with p5. It has built in lighting functions, which offers potential for easily creating gradients or more complex color schemes. My group agreed that this approach had a lot of potential, so we decided to keep exploring it.

Palette

While I worked on code, my group partners worked on developing a draft palette for the different moods and states of Paus. From left to right, the colors represent neutral, alarmed, sad, and happy states.

Iteration 2

In the process of trying to code a rippling sphere, I first coded a custom sphere following this tutorial from Dan Schiffman. While I was able to create and manipulate it, I realized that the built-in lighting functions don't render properly with this method. I also still wasn't satisfied with the motion.

WEBGL texture
