Typeface Spread: Bickham Script

InDesign

"Write a three paragraph statement that highlights the unique characteristics and personality of your typeface in the context of its use. Using your written text, create a two-page spread that introduces the reader to the typeface. Incorporate at least one image, but no more than three."

—

Writing

I did a bit of research, and found that Bickham Script has a rich history that extends back to centuries ago. In summary, the creator of the typeface, Richard Lipton, found a book called "The Universal Penman" by chance while in a library, and fell in love with the calligraphic script within it. That script was written in the 1800s by a master calligrapher named George Bickham; Lipton's typeface is his own tribute to the Bickham's intricate work. There are a lot more details; read the text in the spread for the whole story.

Thumbnails

After the text was set, I did some thumbnail sketches onto small printouts of a 5-column grid to start brainstorming about formatting.

Moodboard

Gathered images and inspiration for possible graphics to use in my spread.

Iteration

Many of these iterations were taken directly from my thumbnails. I worked to try and match my spread with the characteristics of my assigned typeface: flowing, formal, elegant, embellished, established, playful, and dramatic.

Refinement

I narrowed my focus down to this layout, because I felt like it reflected my typeface's characteristics the most.
