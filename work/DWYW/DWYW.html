DWYW — composting campagin

Illustrator, InDesign, HTML/CSS

partners: Sophia Fan, Stefano Didonato

Create a design system, comprised of three design pieces, that encourages and aids composting. The design system must include a large-scale piece (at least 30 x 40 in); a responsive, multi-page webpage; and a physical handheld piece (such as a bag, envelope, or set of stickers).

—

Brainstorming

As a team, we were asked to brainstorm what kind of adjectives we wanted our system to have, and try to visualize them. The adjectives we came up with were: playful, helpful, approachable, engaging, fresh, and clean. We wanted to avoid the forceful, guilt-tripping methods many green campaigns often take, and instead aim for a playful and encouraging mood.

Moodboarding

Based on these adjectives and the overall mood we wanted to go for, we started moodboarding for visual language.

Refining Style

After moodboarding, we weren’t sure if we wanted to go for an illustrative, photographic, or vectorized style. We decided to each try testing illustrative and vectorized styles before deciding.
In the end, we opted for an illustrative style over a vectorized style, because we felt like it aligned more with our goals of being friendly, relatable, and engaging. We decided to combine Sophia's sketches with my coloring, and narrowed down our moodboard.

Slogans

Our team brainstormed together for the main slogans, or catches, of our campaign/system. Our most promising ideas:
  • Breaking down the break down
  • Breaking it down
  • Don’t waste your waste
  • Fill the land, not landfills

Type

We started exploring different fonts we could use, especially pairings between titles and body text. Generally, we wanted a header font that stood out and fit with our theme of friendliness and approachability, and a sans-serif body font that was clean and easy to read.

Header fonts: Chivo, Homemade Apple, Reenie Beanie, Permanent Marker.
