UN Sustainability Goals — video

After Effects

"Create a video of at least 30 seconds which promotes action for one of the UN Sustainable Development Goals. Design your video according to the design guidelines of the UN campaign."

—

Moodboard

After looking through all the goals, I decided that I wanted to focus on goal 14: life below water. I've always seen the ocean as magical, and I would hope that future generations get to experience it and the life that lives within it as well. With this in mind, I started gathering inspiration for possible visual directions i could take. See the full board here.

Approach

While trying to brainstorm the most time-effective methods for creating an animation, I considered using code (p5/WEBGL) as a medium, and made a few tests to see if I could mimic an underwater scene. Though the results were promising, I eventually decided not to use code, since I was worried that my general unfamiliarity with WEBGL would make me lag behind.

Storyboard

My overall concept at this point involved showcasing the beauty of marine life before transitioning into a relevant statistic and call to action. I wanted to achieve the first step with frame-by-frame animation and liquid motion, so I storyboarded that along with different ideas for my ending message.

Iteration 1

After getting feedback, I changed my concept to be less literal and more emotional. Playing with the idea of showing less, I explored a new visual direction: blurring and colorizing stock footage. This allowed me to work a lot faster than I would've been if I was animating, and also created a unique dream-like aesthetic which worked quite well for my concept. I changed my narrative to a slow fade out of sound and footage (a metaphor for the death/extinction of marine species).

Refinement

After receiving more feedback, I realized that my concept was too metaphorical. To make the experience more personal and relatable, I decided to include a narrative. I interviewed friends about their favorite memories of the ocean and pieced together a story from it. I also changed the color palette to include multiple colors after people commented on being confused why the color was muddy.

For the second iteration in this section, I increased the font size and made transitions slower to improve legibility. I later changed to a bolder typeface, slowed transitions further, and fine tuned effects.
